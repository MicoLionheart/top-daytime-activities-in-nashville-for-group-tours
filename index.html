Top Daytime Activities in Nashville for Group Tours

Sharing my favorite daytime activities in Nashville that are perfect for group tours, offering fun, culture, and unforgettable memories.

1. Explore the Country Music Hall of Fame

A Must-Visit for Music Lovers

One of my favorite stops in Nashville is the Country Music Hall of Fame. It’s a treasure trove of music history, showcasing iconic artifacts, exhibits, and stories that celebrate the legends of country music. Whether you’re a lifelong fan or just curious about Nashville’s music roots, this place is a must-see.

For group tours, the Hall of Fame offers guided experiences that make the visit even more engaging. It’s a great way to kick off your day in Music City!

2. Take a Stroll Through Centennial Park

Relax and Enjoy the Outdoors

Whenever I need a break from the hustle and bustle, I head to Centennial Park. This beautiful green space is perfect for group tours, offering plenty of room to relax, take photos, and enjoy the outdoors. The highlight, of course, is the full-scale replica of the Parthenon, which is a stunning sight to see.

It’s a peaceful spot to unwind and soak in some of Nashville’s charm before heading to your next destination.

3. Visit the Johnny Cash Museum

Step Into the Life of a Legend

If you’re a fan of Johnny Cash—or even if you’re not—the Johnny Cash Museum is an incredible experience. It’s packed with memorabilia, interactive exhibits, and personal stories that give you a glimpse into the life of one of music’s greatest icons.

For group tours, this museum is a hit because it’s compact yet packed with fascinating displays. It’s a quick but memorable stop that everyone will enjoy.

4. Enjoy a Food Tour in 12 South

A Culinary Adventure

One of the best ways to experience Nashville is through its food, and the 12 South neighborhood is the perfect place to do it. From hot chicken to gourmet donuts, this area has something for everyone. I love joining a food tour here because it combines delicious bites with stories about the neighborhood’s history and culture.

It’s a fun and tasty way to bond with your group while exploring one of Nashville’s trendiest areas.

5. Take a Downtown Walking Tour

Discover the Heart of Nashville

Whenever I have friends visiting, I always recommend a Downtown Walking Tour. It’s the best way to explore Nashville’s iconic landmarks, like Broadway, the Ryman Auditorium, and the historic Printers Alley. The guides are fantastic and share stories that bring the city’s history to life.

For group tours, this is a fantastic way to get a feel for the city and its vibrant energy.

6. Visit the Ryman Auditorium

The Mother Church of Country Music

No trip to Nashville is complete without a visit to the Ryman Auditorium. This historic venue is steeped in music history and has hosted some of the biggest names in entertainment. Whether you take a guided tour or just stop by to admire the architecture, the Ryman is a must-see.

It’s a great addition to any group tour itinerary, offering a mix of history, culture, and music.
